Planckian srl

Scaling superconducting qubits while preserving performance is one of quantum computing’s toughest challenges.

We go beyond traditional brute-force scaling by decoupling control from qubit count, enabling quantum computers to scale efficiently by design.

A Radical Approach to Scalability

Today’s quantum computers rely on dedicated control lines for each qubit, resulting in dense wiring that degrades qubit performance, inflates system footprint and cost, and fundamentally limits scalability.

Most solutions to wiring problems demand new hardware and nontrivial engineering, making scalability harder, not easier.

We’re charting a new path.

Our unique chip architecture uses shared control lines, drastically reducing wiring complexity while sidestepping the limits of conventional designs.

This approach enables a new generation of quantum computing systems — built on the most advanced qubit platforms and designed to scale from day one.

Powered by a New Way to Compute

At the heart of our innovation lies a new control protocol — a new way to address and operate a qubit.

Unlike traditional processors, in our chip all qubits are addressed simultaneously.

Through innovative engineering of qubit couplings, we exploit these globally broadcasted signals to selectively perform gate operations and move quantum information within the chip.

We rethink information processing from first principles to increase hardware efficiency and fast-track the path to scalable, error-corrected, application-ready quantum computers.
