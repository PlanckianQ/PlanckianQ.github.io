add_factor_node()¶

Create a new factor node connecting the currently selected DOFs.

Notes

  • A factor node represents a Hamiltonian interaction term connecting two or more DOFs.

  • Requires at least two DOFs to be selected.

  • Prevents creation of duplicate factors with identical DOF sets.

  • Automatically places the factor node at the geometric center (in world coordinates) of the selected DOFs, and adjusts position to avoid overlap.

Raises:

  • tkinter.messagebox.showinfo – If fewer than two DOFs are selected.

  • tkinter.messagebox.showwarning – If a factor between the same DOFs already exists.

show_hamiltonian()¶

Display and manage all Hamiltonian interaction terms in the layout.

Opens an interactive window listing all interactions (Hamiltonian terms) grouped by their corresponding factor nodes. The interface allows users to view, select, analyze, rescale, and simplify the coupling terms of the current architecture.

Features¶

  • Display: Shows all defined interaction terms grouped by factor.

  • Selection: Click lines to highlight or select specific interactions.

  • Parameter inspection: Displays parametric couplings with defaults.

  • Editing:

    • Group duplicate terms with identical operators and DOFs.

    • Simplify (remove zero-strength terms).

    • Apply Gaussian disorder or rescaling to numeric couplings.

    • Rewrite a, a† operators as σ⁺/σ⁻ or expand σ±σ∓ to Pauli terms.

  • Statistical tools:

    • Compute ⟨|J|⟩ (mean absolute coupling) and standard deviation σ.

    • Plot histogram of |J| distribution for selected couplings.

  • Undo support: All destructive operations (simplify, rescale, etc.) trigger snapshot_layout() for full undo safety.

Notes

  • Parametric (symbolic) terms are not modified by rescaling or disorder.

  • Non-Hermitian terms can optionally be highlighted in red if CONSIDER_NONHERMITIAN = True.

  • The view updates automatically after every change.

  • Interaction removal automatically updates the parametric map.

See also

rewrite_products_to_paulis

Expand σ± products into Pauli Z and I components.
